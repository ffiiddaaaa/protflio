I have been writing since the age of thirteen. I never stopped and I never knew how to stop.
Poetry consumed me.
When I couldn't use my words, I wrote. It is a form of therapy. It heals.
You can write the deepest and saddest poems, yet you'll see yourself lighter. You wouldn't be carrying all the weight in your heart.
Poetry is a beautiful thing. Write. Decorate with it. Give love. For many centuries it had been admired and respected.
For the reason that it is one of the most precious forms of art. My mother had been my biggest supporter when it came to poetry.
She kept a couple of pieces she had wrote. But never pursuit it.

Not Just a Poem

you-
aren't just a poem 
you are a haiku in the wind 
a golden stroke on my skin
you are my jasmine in the summer 
my violet in the sky 
my shooting star in a dark night 
you are the shinning sun 
that makes me bloom

Wooden Stools

like a lonely wooden stool 
like this melting cube in my whiskey 
you walk my empty heart
as if you own it 
and indeed, you do
like a dying ladybug 
I sit here poking at my wristwatch
watching the days 
I am yearning
